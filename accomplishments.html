Accomplishments

IEEE Accomplishments                                 

IEEE Computer Society:
- Served as a member of Diversity & Inclusion Task Force 2020 and the Diversity and Inclusion Committee 2021 and contributed towards activities of the committee.
- Served as Women in Engineering Representative 2019 and 2020 and organised the Returning Mothers Conference 2019 and 2020
- Served as 2020 WIE Governance Adhoc Committee Chair and proposed for electing the WIE Chair and Treasurer. It was approved by the MGA leadership and the 2021 Annual Election Ballot contains slate for WIE Chair election
IEEE Society on Social Implications of Technology
- Volunteered on the Chapters Committee from 2015 and became the Chapters Committee Chair in 2017. Introduced the Chapters Webinar Series in 2018 to bring together the chapters.
- The dormant chapters were given mentorship and three chapters which were on the verge of dissolution were revived 
- Increased the number of student branch chapters from 3  to 6 and the chapters from 23 to 26
IEEE Women in Engineering:
- Introduced the WIE Celebration projects like WIE posters and WIE E Book where the achievements of the members were published
- Organised the First Women in Engineering Conference with the support of Google in 2013 which was a precursor to the IEEE Women in Engineering Leadership Conference
- Introduced the idea of Women in Engineering Leadership Summits all over the world and organised the first pilot WIE Leadership Summit in 2015 in India
- Serving as the WIE Adhoc Committee Chair for WIE Governance for encouraging more empowerment for the WIE Committee members in IEEE
IEEE Region 10:
- Introduced and organised the First Women in Engineering Congress along with the Student and Young Professional Congress in 2008. Took over as the Region 10 WIE Coordinator for Women in Engineering in 2008 and brought the status of Region 10 WIE from dormant to the most active and the biggest in the world. Organised International WIE Congresses in various places subsequently apart from the Region 10 Congress.
- Introduced two awards - IEEE WIE Most Inspiring Member Award, IEEE WIE Affinity Group of the Year Award to encourage the active Affinity Groups (AG) and the individuals. The Most Inspiring Member Award was later introduced in the Global Committee. 
- Introduced the Women in Engineering tracks in the Region 10 flagship conferences - TENCON, Tensymp and this tradition is followed till now
- Introduced the Sangamam Project which gained a worldwide popularity since it is for transferring Technology for the Rural and Underserved areas. This brought in the Need based Technology Activities under Women in Engineering to the world wide popularity as Humanitarian Technology
-Increased the 44 AG strength Region 10 WIE in 2008 to 129 AG strength in 2010 which was three times the 
IEEE Madras Section, R10:
- Joined as Excom member in 2004 and introduced the first Faculty Development Program under Madras Section
- Founded the Madras IEEE Women in Engineering AG, Madras IEEE Education Society Chapter, Madras IEEE SIGHT and Madras IEEE SSIT and all the four are active till date
- Proposed the First Women in Engineering Congress to Region 10 in 2007 and organised it in 2008. This has become regular in Region 10 and is followed to date.
- As treasurer of IEEE Madras Section in 2009, formed the funding regulations for the travels, events and other activities of the section which was not available till then.

Non-IEEE Accomplishments

- Anita Borg Institute for Women and Technology:
- I was honoured with the ABI Change Agent Award in 2012 in Baltimore Grace Hopper Celebrations for my service to women and other underserved communities. 
- Grace Hopper Celebration India was just one year old when I took over as the Student Career Fair and Scholarship Chair in 2013 -2016 and built up the whole ecosystem for selecting the students for the scholarships provided by the Fortune 500 companies and other sponsors. 
  I served on the Advisory Committee of Grace Hopper India from 2012 to 2016 and provided the important linkage between the IEEE Women in Engineering and ABI for collaboration
- Introduced the first Returning Mothers Workshop with the award I received from Systers Community of ABI which encourages passing the kindness to other underserved communities - in this case women. 
- Discover E:
- I served in the Thought Leaders of Global Marathon, which is a worldwide virtual conference from 2014 to 2018 and contributed to the success of the conference by providing world class speakers and by organising Physical Coffee Chats. These chats provided a platform for the local participants to discuss the various issues provided by the speakers and the inferences were given as feed back to the organisers. 
- I contributed to a book chapter on "Internet of Women" edited by the Thought leaders which gave a colourful view of status of career women in culturally different backgrounds.
